Vahnaree

Vahnaree Weis is an undergraduate student at the School of the Art Institute of Chicago, specializing in Fiber and Material Studies and Art Therapy/Education. She transitioned from DePaul University, where she studied Art, Media, and Design with a focus on Studio. Vahnaree is actively involved in the arts community through her work at the DePaul Art Museum and The Wasteshed. She is also the co-founder of Eden’s Flora, a local artist collective dedicated to enhancing the visibility and accessibility of Chicago’s artistic community. Her experience includes internships with various non-profits, such as Arts of Life, where she served as an artist aid, and The Wasteshed, where she currently works as the SAIC Digital Marketing and Creative Reuse Assistant Intern. Her recent research trip to Ecuador, centered on the extinction crisis, has deeply influenced her work, of which explores both societal and personal themes.
